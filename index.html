    Research Assistant at Texas A&M University

    Short Biography

    I am a research assistant at the Power Systems Automation Laboratory at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering of the Texas A&M University. Under the guidance of Prof. Emmanuel Frimpong, I completed my Bachelor's degree in the College of Engineering at the same university in November 2023. I worked as a teaching assistant there during that time as well. Before joining the department as a teaching assistant, I undertook my national service as a research assistant at the Information Security and Technology Department at UITS. I aided in the establishment of a Security Operating Center for the University and served as a Trainee SOC analyst for the latter half of my stay. Between June and September 2023, I also earned several relevant cybersecurity certifications with help from UITS, including Microsoft SC-900 and Google Cybersecurity Certificate. From September 2023 to October 2024, I joined the K.N.U.S.T Power Systems laboratory to research applications of computational techniques in power systems. In January 2025, I officially became an Aggie! I am pursuing my PhD under the esteemed Prof. Karen Butler-Purry!'s supervision. My ultimate ambition is to work on futuristic problems, and to build solutions that will impact the post-modern age of power systems, cities, and digital services.

    Research Interests

    I am interested in maximizing the potential of artificial intelligence in power networks, especially as the smart grid era beckons. My interests encompass various facets of power systems, including protection, cybersecurity, automation, control, planning, and optimization. As I continue researching, I aim to expand my focus to networked and multi-agent systems, addressing broader optimization and grid resilience challenges.

    My research interests fall into four (4) macro-areas of Electrical and Computer Engineering:

    • Machine Learning

    • Security and Privacy

    • Control and Optimization

    • Anomaly Detection

    Domains of application include:

    • Power Systems/Smart Grids

    • Networked/Cyber-physical Systems

    • Electrical Machines

    • Smart Systems/Cities
